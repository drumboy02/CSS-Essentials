Trivia

A small waterfall with a glowing flame inside Hidden within Chestnut Ridge Park in New York, Eternal Flame Falls is one of nature’s most intriguing sights. A small, naturally occurring flame burns at the base of the waterfall, fueled by a tiny gas seep beneath the rock. Scientists believe the flame was ignited centuries ago—possibly by Native Americans—and continues to flicker today.
A fiery crater glowing intensely in the desert In the Karakum Desert of Turkmenistan, there’s a massive crater called The Door to Hell—and it has been burning continuously for over 50 years! Originally a collapsed gas field, the fire was lit to prevent the spread of methane, but instead, it has created one of the most surreal landscapes on Earth.
A village with waterways instead of streets The picturesque town of Giethoorn in the Netherlands is often called the "Venice of the North." What makes it special? There are no roads! Instead, residents and visitors travel by boat along serene canals, surrounded by thatched-roof cottages and lush gardens.
A grove of oddly curved trees Deep in western Poland lies the Crooked Forest, a bizarre collection of pine trees that all mysteriously bend at their trunks. No one knows exactly why they grow this way—some say it’s due to human intervention, while others believe it’s a natural phenomenon. Whatever the reason, it remains one of nature’s strangest wonders.
